How it Works
• Coverage: If one buoy is lost, neighbours take the load – correct spacing prevents chain failures.
• Cable Weight in Water: Effective submerged mass per metre.
• Buoyancy: Usable lift of each float (realistic working value).
• Spacing: Centre-to-centre distance between floats – sets how much cable each float supports.
• Safety Factor: SF ≥ 1 means safety margin. SF < 1 (down to 0.5) means deliberate under-float, cable will ride lower in the water.
